| Material                   | Role in PV Module                                                                                                                    | Key Properties                                                                                                                 | Recycling Considerations                                                                                                                                  |
|:---------------------------|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Solar Glass [^3][^2]       | Front protective layer; provides rigidity, high optical transmission, physical protection from the elements.                         | Low Fe composition; Tempered for durability; Chemically stable; High transmissivity; High melting point.                       | High mass but low value; EVA separation is the main barrier to recovery.                                                                                  |
| Copper [^2]                | Primary conductor in PV interconnection. Used in busbars & internal wiring. Potential to replace silver in solar cell metallization. | High strength & hardness; Great electrical and heat conductivity                                                               | Often tightly bonded to other materials, which can hinder recycling process; EVA layer is also a barrier to accessing the silicon wafer and other metals. |
| EVA (Encapsulant) [^2][^6] | Encapsulates and protects cells from atmospheric influences, vibrations, and humidity; bonds module layers together.                 | Durable at high temperatures; High bonding strength; Cross‑linked polymer; Flexible; Difficult to delaminate during recycling. | Major barrier to recycling due to bonding and cross-linking; Requires thermal or chemical removal.                                                        |
| Low-Carbon Steel [^5][^9]  | Used in mounting hardware, racking, and some structural components.                                                                  | High strength; Highly malleable & ductile; Widely recyclable with established infrastructure.                                  | Highly recyclable; Contributes to circularity in mounting systems.                                                                                        |
| Aluminum [^2][^4][^9]      | Commonly used for module frames due to high strength-to-weight ratio.                                                                | Lightweight; Corrosion‑resistant; High embodied energy; Non-toxic; Excellent recyclability.                                    | Using aluminum scrap can reduce CO2 emissions by 92% compared to the raw element; Easily recovered and recycled.                                          |
